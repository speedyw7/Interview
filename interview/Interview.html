Interview with a student

Interviewer: Why do you want to be a designer?

Student: I want to create many cool pieces of art for myself and companies and be able to go into the art side of the game industry.

Interviewer: What makes you nervous about becoming a designer?

Student: The many exspectations that come with it.

Interviewer: What excites you about design?

Student: Getting the chance to use my imagination to create new things

Interviewer: What are your strengths?

Student: I work really well with tech.

Interviewer: What are some areas of opportunity to work on?

Student: my 2D digital art skills.

Interviewer: What aspects of graphic design to you gravitate towards?

Student: Handdrawning with painting, ink work and 3D modeling.

Interviewer: What are the roles and responsibilities of a graphic design in society?

Student: To complete your task and deliver something the consumer wants.

Interviewer: When/where/why are you at your most creative/productive?

Student: When i let my mind flow free and dont think about any worry in the world.

Interviewer: What are your comfort levels with giving and receiving feedback? How might you improve your skills here? What strategies have worked for you?

Student: My comfort levels are very comfortable as i always enjoy any feedback as it can help me improve. I would seek knowledge on what i need to help me improve and then work towards it. Lots and lots of drawing.

Interviewer: What is a website?

Student: A website can be anything you want on the web.
